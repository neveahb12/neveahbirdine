Phishing, Flaming, and Spamming

Phishing is when hackers try to get information by posing as trustworthy services such as a bank. Normally they’ll come to you in a sense of urgency in hopes to get you panicked so you’ll do what they say without second guessing. Spamming (usually emails) are mass messages sent to many different account with the hopes that someone would fall for it. They sound ridiculous so you expect no one to believe it. However they’re sent so various people who can be unversed in the world of spam. 30% of people open these emails and out of that 30, 18% click on the malicious links attached. Flaming is a barrage of rude messages targeted at a specific individual. It usually take place in comment sections and forums where someone’s opinion differs from the flamer.
